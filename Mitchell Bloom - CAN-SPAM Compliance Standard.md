# Mitchell Bloom — CAN-SPAM Compliance Standard

**Status:** Governing standard. **All cold email copy for this client must comply with every rule below before it is sent.**
**Primary source:** FTC, *CAN-SPAM Act: A Compliance Guide for Business* — <https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/can-spam-act-compliance-guide-business> (guide edited Jan 2024 for inflation-adjusted penalties).
**Applies to:** every campaign and every email step (E1–E4), every A/B variant, across all ICPs (stockholders, trophy homes, business sellers).

---

## Why this matters here
- **Penalty: up to $53,088 per individual email in violation.** Each email is a separate violation, so a single non-compliant sequence sent at volume is an existential legal risk.
- **B2B is not exempt.** The Act covers *all* commercial messages ("primary purpose … commercial advertisement or promotion of a commercial product or service"). Cold outreach to owners, employees, and homeowners is commercial and fully in scope.
- **Liability is shared and cannot be contracted away.** Both the company whose service is promoted (**Bloom Financial**) *and* the company that sends the mail (**Lead Gen Jay**) can be held legally responsible. "Monitor what others are doing on your behalf" is an explicit FTC requirement.
- **This program is also under Colorado RIA ad-review.** CAN-SPAM is the federal floor; Mitch's RIA compliance rules (non-promissory, disclaimer on every email) sit **on top** of it. Copy must clear both.

## Transactional vs. commercial (why we can't shortcut the footer)
Our cold emails are **commercial** — their primary purpose is promoting Bloom Financial's service. They do **not** qualify for the "transactional or relationship" carve-out (which is narrow: fulfilling an agreed transaction, account/warranty/safety notices, membership changes, etc.). Therefore **every** email we send must carry the full commercial requirements: accurate headers, honest subject, ad identification, physical address, and opt-out. There is no "first-touch cold email is exempt" loophole.

---

## Reconciling compliance with cold-email brevity + the goal
The goal of every email is **one thing: get a reply from an interested prospect.** Compliance and brevity are not in conflict if we separate the two zones of the email:

- **Pitch body (the part that earns the reply):** stays short — **under 100 words, target ~75**, 6th-grade reading level, one idea, one soft CTA. Legal text lives *below* the signature so it never competes with the hook.
- **Compliance footer (below the sign-off):** compact block carrying the three required legal elements. It can be small and quiet, but it must be present and legible:

```
Mitchell Bloom
Bloom Financial

[DISCLAIMER: Mitch's RIA compliance verbiage — required before launch]
Bloom Financial, [valid physical postal address]
Reply "unsubscribe" and I'll take you off my list.
```

This keeps the top of the email clean and persuasive while the footer satisfies rules 3, 4, and 5. A reply-based "unsubscribe" is an accepted opt-out mechanism under the Act (a return email address qualifies), and it keeps the required word recipients look for while staying low-friction.

## RIA overlay (Colorado ad-review) — stacks on top of CAN-SPAM
These are additional constraints locked on the July 14 strategy call and in the 2nd analysis:
- **No promissory language or guarantees.** Describe what the structure *does* (defer, schedule, review), never what the prospect *will get*. Everyone's situation differs.
- **Disclaimer on every single email** (this is not tax or investment advice; consult your own CPA/attorney). Mitch supplies the exact verbiage — **launch blocker until received.**
- **Banned from all cold copy:** Rob Lowe's name/story, audit statistics ("X audits, no findings"), audit indemnification, and any pricing/fees.
- **Avoid "tax" and "free" in sending domains** (read as promissory); the words may appear in body copy where accurate.
- **CPA-hero framing:** position the offer as working *with* the prospect's existing CPA, never replacing them.
